What is a mapcode?

A mapcode represents a precise location on Earth.

Context / Territories

The context part of a mapcode represents the area where the mapcode is based.

This will usually be the ISO-3166 standard 3-letter country code.

For example: GBR for United Kingdom, ITA for Italy, etc.

Some countries with separated states can also have a precise context.

This means the mapcode will be shorter and easier to remember.

For example: For New York you can use US-NY instead of USA,
or for New South Wales you can use AU-NSW. AU-NSW DM9.XQ easier to use than AUS RK2M.LM0Q!
